<!-- Example format:

### Out-of-Scope Hallucination
**Definition**: Bot states a confident fact about a topic not covered in the knowledge base, instead of saying "I don't know."
**Pass**: Bot says it doesn't know and directs user to ebay.com/help
**Fail**: Bot invents a plausible-sounding answer (e.g., "eBay Live is accessed via the main eBay app")

### Incorrect Policy Claim
**Definition**: Bot states a policy detail that contradicts the knowledge base.
**Pass**: Policy stated matches the knowledge base exactly
**Fail**: Bot states wrong fee, wrong availability, wrong category, etc.

### Failure to Escalate
**Definition**: Bot gives a confident answer when it should admit uncertainty and escalate.
**Pass**: Bot says "I'm not sure, please check ebay.com/help" for genuinely unknown topics
**Fail**: Bot answers confidently on topics outside its knowledge base

### Wrong User Type Framing
**Definition**: Bot gives buyer-focused advice to a seller question or vice versa.
**Pass**: Response correctly addresses the user's role (buyer or seller)
**Fail**: Response misidentifies or ignores the user's role

### Incomplete Answer
**Definition**: Bot omits a key detail that is clearly present in the knowledge base.
**Pass**: All relevant KB details are included
**Fail**: Bot answers partially, missing an important caveat or step
